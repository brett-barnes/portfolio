Summer Internship

I spent ten weeks solo-developing an Android OS app for an upcoming ADHD research study. This app would generate a series of surveys (5x per day for the course of the study) at randomly generated times within specified intervals, store the data on the phone via a JSON file, and then upload it to a sever via PHP. I utilized background services, PeriodicWorkManager, threading, and AlarmManager to scheduele and deliver the notifications on-time and consistently in a real-world environment (resilient to phone updates, restarts, and resets which ordinarily clear upcoming tasks). In addition to the notifications when the survey opened, uncompleted surveys will recieve reminders. Class Project

In Logisim, a lab partner and I designed a CPU from scratch, building a regilfe, ALU, control, and ROM, and connecting them with the appropriate logic gates and structure. We then expanded functionality into a full computer by adding RAM, a monitor, and a keyboard connected via a bus. We additionally wrote a MIPS Assembly compiler in C++, which would compile into binary, which would then run via our CPU's ROM. The end result was a fully functioning computer which was capable of anything written in MIPS, as well as a variety of additional pseudoinstructions, such as complex mathematics, drawing on the monitor with WASD, or digitally rendering a painting like Starry Night.

Traffic Flow Simulator

C++
|
Class Project

A lab partner and I developed a deterministic coordinate based traffic simulation that took as input a series of probabilities (for example, the proportion of vehicles which would be cars, SUVs, or trucks, or the probability of a new vehicle entering southbound at any given instant) and a seed, used to generate the specific scenario. The same input probabiltiies & seeds will produce the same outcomes. We placed a heavy emphasis on encapsulation and efficiency, resulting in our code being significantly shorter than the other teams', and chose to implement factory method principles to allow reusing of code and avoid redundencies. Vehicles in this simulation will move forward whenever allowed, stopping when they are confronted with a red light, another car, or an unsafe turn (such as a left turn into oncoming traffic), and take up varying amount of space in the cooridnate grid depending on their vehicle type.

Personal Project

Hosted by the NFL and AWS, we were challenged to compute our own advanced statistics, which could be used by an NFL team to adjust their decision making. I computed Defensive and Offensive Apex Tackler Efficiency (DATE, OATE), which measure a defender's ability to bring down hard-to-tackle ball-carriers, and ball-carriers' ability to break tackles by elite defenders. I calculated Forced Missed Tackle Percentage for each ball-carrier, and weighed every tackle by the FMT% of the tackled ball-carrier, and by whether it was solo or assisted. Percentile scores of this weighted metric provided our first stat, DATE. Now, I was able to calculate OATE by weighing every ball-carriers' FMTs by the tackling defender's DATE- a higher score means they broke tackles from defenders who are usually strong in tackling elite ball-carriers. The variety of use cases for this stat are discussed in the readme.txt.
